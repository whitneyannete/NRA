Introduction

Dear Taxpayer,
This is your Audit/Assessment report, issued in accordance with Sections 32 of the Taxation Act, 2009. All Taxpayers and those doing business in South Sudan are required to pay and submit their monthly returns of PIT, and BPT on monthly and quarterly basis respectively at the rates provided in Schedule II, and section (92) of the Taxation Act 2009. Report of taxes withheld and deducted are required to be submitted on a monthly, for PIT and quarterly basis for BPT, by the 15th of every month of, March, June,September and December of every financial year.

Interest

As provided in Section 36 of Taxation Act 2009, interest shall be charged if any part of a tax liability is not paid by the date prescribed for payment. The current interest rate for unpaid taxes is 120% per month above the commercial rate; chargeable at beginning of the day after tax payment was due and chargeable for every month that the tax remains unpaid. Interest is computed after 15th of every Month if the payment of the tax remaining is unpaid.

Additional charges

As provided in Section 38 of Taxation Act 2009, a late filing penalty of 5% is assessed for each month for which the return is late, up to a maximum of 25%. The penalty is calculated on that basis, nonpayment and non-filing, from January 2016 to December 2018.

Late Payment Penalty

As provided in Section 37 of Taxation Act 2009, a penalty for late payment of 5% is charged for each month that payment of a tax liability is not paid. The penalty is calculated from January 2018 to December 2020 for the amount unpaid

Recommendations

The Auditors found from the taxpayer, issues related to lack of awareness of how and when to file and pay the tax. The committee recommend tax awareness campaigns through the following:

  1. Tax seminars and workshops.
  2. Instant text messages through mobile operators as sms to all mobile subscribers.
  3. Taxation manuals
  4. Media houses, TV, Newspapers etc.
  5. Circulars to business community.
